    # Highlights

    • 23 questions to reflect on 2023 (and make 2024 your best year yet): ( View Tweet)
    • This list will help you: • Recap the year • Take stock of where you are • And refresh your perspective heading into 2024 It’s the exact process I used to do my Yearly Review. Let’s dive in: ( View Tweet)
      1. What were the big moments, memories, and milestones from this year? Add some constraints and try to pick 3-5 per month. Write this as if you were writing a summary of the year you could look back on in 20 years. ( View Tweet)
      1. What were my biggest wins & accomplishments of the year? No matter what happened this year, you have things to celebrate. Start this reflection on a positive note by brainstorming everything you did that made you feel: • Proud • Happy • Fulfilled • Grateful ( View Tweet)
      1. What things do I look back on most fondly, that brought me the most energy and joy? • People • Places • Habits • Tasks • Experiences How can I do more of these things in 2024? ( View Tweet)
      1. What things do I look back on less fondly, draining my energy and weighing on me throughout the year? • People • Places • Habits • Tasks • Experiences How can I do fewer of these things in 2024? ( View Tweet)
      1. How could I be a better: • Son/daughter • Employee • Manager • Partner • Mentor • Friend ( View Tweet)
      1. What am I most grateful for in 2023? Or, a better way to phrase this: What do I have today that I would have begged to have had 5 years ago? (Bonus points for keeping a list of things throughout the year you’re grateful for & reviewing it at the end of the year.) ( View Tweet)
      1. What’s on my “best of” list? • Books • Events • Articles • Podcasts • Purchases • New habits Bonus points for writing these up and sharing them with your friends! ( View Tweet)
      1. How did I grow as a person? • New skills • New habits • New priorities • New preferences • New relationships • New perspectives • New environments ( View Tweet)
    • Now from here, split your life into areas and dig into each of them on by one. My current buckets: • Health • Wealth • Relationships • Experiences • Business • Operations Then you can ask the following questions in each area: ( View Tweet)
      1. What were my biggest achievements in this area? And what are the 20% of things that led to this achievement? • People • Beliefs • Routines • Priorities • Environments How can I double down on these in 2024? ( View Tweet)
      1. Where did I make mistakes or fall short in this area? And what are the 20% of things that contributed to me falling short? • People • Beliefs • Routines • Priorities • Environments How can I stop doing these things in 2024? ( View Tweet)
      1. Which area of my life received the most attention? And which area of my life received the least? Is this the right allocation of my time, energy, and attention? ( View Tweet)
      1. Based on these questions, what do I want to: • Stop doing in 2024 • Start doing in 2024 • Continue doing in 2024 Get creative here, brain dumping as many answers as you can think of. Then, prioritize them and focus on 1 at a time. ( View Tweet)
    • Alright, that’s enough of looking backward. Now for some more general reflection questions to get the creative juices flowing: ( View Tweet)
      1. What’s the single biggest bottleneck to my continued growth? And why aren’t I relentlessly focused on removing it? ( View Tweet)
      1. If I had unlimited money, how would I invest today to increase my time, energy, or happiness? • $10? • $100? • $500? • $10,000? ( View Tweet)
      1. If I repeated my weekly actions every week for 5 years, where would I end up? Is this where I want to end up? ( View Tweet)
      1. Love this one from James Clear: Imagine your career is a game & ask questions like: • What game am I playing? • What are the rules of the game? • How do players advance? • How do players lose? • Is this game still serving me? ( View Tweet)
      1. What are some things I could start doing today that would guarantee an absolutely horrible year? And how can I avoid doing those things? ( View Tweet)
      1. Where am I taking myself too seriously? And where am I making things harder than they need to be? ( View Tweet)
      1. How am I complicit in creating the conditions I say I don’t want? (Credit to Jerry Colonna and Tim Ferriss on this one.) ( View Tweet)
      1. With my finite resources: • Time • Energy • Attention • Capital Am I “spending” them? Or am I investing them? ( View Tweet)
      1. What were the 5 most difficult conversations of the year? And what are 5 difficult conversations I’m currently avoiding (with both myself and others)? ( View Tweet)
      1. How could I achieve my 10-year goals in 1 year? And similarly, how could I achieve 10x my 1-year goals this year? ( View Tweet)
      1. What am I saying “no” to in the year ahead? And what will that free up time for me to say “yes” to? ( View Tweet)
